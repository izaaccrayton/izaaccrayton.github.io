The Streaming Revolution: Gaming’s Next Frontier

From floppy disks to now buying games with a click of a button in your own home, the way video game products being sold is changing rapidly

By Crystal Chen on May 4th, 2019

The gaming industry has become one of the most evolved and complex entertainments provided in this era of technology. From floppy disks to now buying games with a click of a button in your own home, the way video game products being sold have been more widely available and easy to obtain. But now in the same steps of how music and media entertainment has changed its product distribution, the gaming world is now following in its footsteps, digital streaming. 

In 2019 GDC (Game Developers Conference), Google announced Stadia, a cloud gaming service they have developed. Targeting gamers with the appeal of having access to many games without the demand of high-performance CPU. To have games without the need to download and always accessible with only a few clicks, games themselves can now be streamed to their servers, only requiring a wifi connection. 

Not only is it aimed for gamers but also developers and youtube content creators, allowing them to publish their own creations for others to play and unlimited content to be created for others to view. The endless possibilities of being able to play millions of video games with any type of laptop, phone or tablet in HD quality of 1080p without the need of a gaming pc, Xbox, ps4 or any other console. 

Similar companies have already started advertising the concept of game streaming. Shadow, a game service, was the first to sell a service that gives you access to a full gaming PC via streaming from their servers. Others are following the highly-anticipated tread of streaming such as Microsoft. Announcing one of their key projects, Project xCloud, their cloud gaming service. 

If Stadia succeeds in their aspirations, this could become the new method of purchasing games on a single platform. The Streaming revolution has shaped our future of accessing multiple sources of media. The Gaming World will now be open to different ranges of audiences. Similar to Music being streamed with SoundCloud and Spotify or Movies/TV shows with Hulu and Netflix, this could become the next hugest investment. Gathering more consumers and audiences to gain the profit of another source of entertainment for all ages.

Crystal Chen
